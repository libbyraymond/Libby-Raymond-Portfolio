Basement Buggy

This automated track system addresses the daily challenge of basement access for an elderly client living with Parkinson's disease and osteoarthritis. The device eliminates the need for navigating stairs while carrying items, reducing fall risk and physical strain through an engineered mechanical solution.

I designed and built a custom track system featuring a motorized basket attachment capable of transporting up to 10 pounds between floors. The system automates what was previously a dangerous and painful task, allowing the client to send and retrieve items from the basement independently without stair navigation. The design prioritized reliability, safety, and ease of use. These were critical considerations for a user with limited dexterity and mobility. This project demonstrates how targeted assistive technology can preserve independence and quality of life by addressing specific environmental barriers that impact daily living activities.
