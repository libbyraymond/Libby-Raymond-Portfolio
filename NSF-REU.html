NSF Research

This National Science Foundation Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU) project investigated sustainable transportation solutions to enhance campus connectivity at the University of Delaware. The study focused on designing a safe, efficient, and effective Net-Zero People Mover by evaluating four distinct transportation modes and their potential impact on campus mobility.

I designed comprehensive evaluation matrices to systematically assess each transportation solution, analyzing existing traffic patterns and connectivity challenges across campus. Working collaboratively with state, city, and university officials, I helped align the project scope with broader transportation objectives and ensure feasibility within regulatory frameworks. The research culminated in a formal report submitted to the NSF REU program and a research poster presentation that communicated findings to academic and administrative stakeholders.
