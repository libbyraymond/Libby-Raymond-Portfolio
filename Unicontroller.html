Unicontroller

The Unicontroller is a custom-designed left-handed video game console created for a patient with limited mobility on their right side. This assistive technology device reimagines traditional controller ergonomics, enabling independent gaming through innovative mechanical design and personalized adaptability.

My team of four Northwestern students, designed and built a specialized controller featuring lever arm mechanisms that translate left-handed inputs into right-side controller functions, effectively mirroring traditional gameplay controls. The device includes an adjustable positioning arm that accommodates individual comfort needs and varying mobility limitations, ensuring extended play sessions remain ergonomic. Using 3D printing technology, we rapidly prototyped and iterated on the design, creating a fully functional assistive device tailored to the user's specific needs. This project demonstrates how custom fabrication and thoughtful mechanical design can enhance accessibility and independence for users with mobility challenges, transforming recreational activities that were previously inaccessible into achievable experiences. Our solution allowed our user to play FIFA, independently for the first time in years.
